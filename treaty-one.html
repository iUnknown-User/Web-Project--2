Treaty 1

Which communities were involved in the signing of Treaty 1?

These communities were involved in forming Treaty One: the Anishinaabe, Muskegon Cree peoples, and the Crown.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_1

About the communities

The Anishinaabe originated on the northeast coast of what is now Canada and the United States.

The Muskegon were native-born Americans from New England, New York, and Pennsylvania.

The Crown refers to the royals from England.

https://www.canada.ca/en/crown-indigenous-relations-northern-affairs/services/treaties.html

What were the terms of the Treaty 1 agreement?

In Treaty One, the band of Yellow Quill would receive a further 25 square miles of land to be laid out around the reserve.

The Indigenous peoples were granted 160 acres of land per family.

https://www.rcaanc-cirnac.gc.ca/eng/1100100028574/1581294169921

What makes Treaty 1 different from other treaties?

In the final agreement of Treaty 1, Indigenous peoples were to hand over most of their land in southeast and south-central Manitoba to Governor Archibald and the Crown.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_1

How is the treaty being implemented today?

An example of Treaty 1's current implementation is the Kapyong urban reserve, created after a longstanding negotiation between the government and First Nations.
